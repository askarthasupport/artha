Privacy & Safe Usage Guide for Artha

Last Updated: July 2026

Artha is designed as a privacy-first, offline-first personal finance, wealth tracking, reminder, and secure records application. This guide explains how Artha handles your information, what security practices are followed, what choices you control, and how you can use the app safely.

This page is intended to help users understand safe usage. It should be read together with Artha's Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions.

Your Money. Your Device. Your Control.

Artha is built to help you manage expenses, income, budgets, investments, goals, reminders, family records, and important documents while keeping your information under your control.

By default, your information is stored locally on your Android device. Artha does not require bank linking, SMS reading, internet banking access, or developer-operated cloud storage for normal use.

Security Practices

Artha uses a privacy-conscious and security-aware design approach. Depending on the features you choose to use, Artha may include:

  • Local storage by default.
  • Android private application storage.
  • Local database storage such as Room Database where applicable.
  • PIN protection where available.
  • Biometric unlock where available.
  • Encrypted Vault storage where supported.
  • Encrypted local backups where supported.
  • No mandatory account registration for core offline use.
  • No bank account linking.
  • No SMS reading for financial tracking.
  • No collection of UPI PINs, card PINs, ATM PINs, OTPs, internet banking passwords, or similar sensitive credentials.

No app, device, or electronic storage method can guarantee absolute security. Artha is designed to reduce risk, but your device security, backup handling, and sharing choices also play an important role.

Permissions Used

Artha requests only the permissions required for features you choose to use.

  • Camera access may be used when you choose to capture documents, receipts, or images.
  • File or storage access may be used when you choose to import, export, back up, or restore files.
  • Notification permission may be used for reminders, alerts, due dates, and important app notifications.
  • Biometric authentication may be used when you choose to enable biometric unlock.

Permissions are requested only when required by a feature and can be denied or revoked through your Android device settings. Some features may not work properly if required permissions are denied.

Privacy & Safe Usage Guide

To use Artha safely, we recommend that you:

  • Use a strong device screen lock.
  • Enable biometric authentication or app PIN where available.
  • Keep your Android device updated.
  • Install apps only from trusted sources.
  • Avoid using rooted, modified, or compromised devices.
  • Create encrypted backups regularly where supported.
  • Store backups in a safe and trusted location.
  • Review exported reports before sharing them.
  • Do not share sensitive documents through untrusted apps, links, websites, or messaging channels.
  • Verify important financial information before making financial decisions.

Your Choices and Control

You decide what information you enter, store, edit, export, back up, share, or delete inside Artha.

You control:

  • What financial records you add.
  • What documents you store in Vault.
  • What reminders you create.
  • Whether to export reports.
  • Whether to create backups.
  • Where backups are stored.
  • Whether to share exported files with others.
  • Whether to delete local app data.

Artha does not review, verify, monitor, or modify your personal financial records.

Accuracy of Information

Artha relies on the information entered by you.

Reports, reminders, budgets, calculations, insights, summaries, and financial views are generated based on the records available inside the app.

If information is missing, outdated, duplicated, or entered incorrectly, reports and summaries may also be inaccurate. You are responsible for reviewing and verifying your records.

Automated Insights

Any trends, summaries, recommendations, reminders, forecasts, or analytical insights generated by Artha are based on the information available within the application.

These insights are intended to help with personal organization and awareness. They should not be treated as financial, investment, legal, tax, insurance, or accounting advice.

Content Stored in Vault

Documents, images, medical records, insurance papers, warranties, certificates, family records, and other information stored in Vault remain under your control.

You are responsible for ensuring that you have the legal right to store, access, export, or share any information kept within Vault.

You should avoid storing or sharing information belonging to others unless you have appropriate permission or authority to do so.

Exporting and Sharing Information

Artha may allow you to export reports, share files, create backups, or send information using other apps installed on your device.

These actions happen only when you choose to perform them.

Once information leaves Artha through export, sharing, email, messaging, cloud storage, file transfer, or any other third-party app, its handling is controlled by that third party and is outside Artha's control.

External Links and Third-Party Apps

Artha may include links to support email, Play Store pages, legal pages, or other external resources.

When you click an external link or use a third-party app, that service may collect or process information according to its own privacy policy and terms.

Please review links carefully before opening them or sharing information.

If Your Device Is Lost or Compromised

Because Artha stores information locally by default, the safety of your data depends partly on the safety of your device.

If your device is lost, stolen, rooted, infected with malware, physically accessed by someone else, or otherwise compromised, locally stored information may become exposed or inaccessible.

Since Artha does not maintain developer-operated cloud copies of your personal records, the developer cannot remotely lock, erase, recover, or restore your local data.

Backups and Recovery

Artha may allow you to create encrypted local backups where supported.

You are responsible for creating, storing, and protecting your backups.

If your device is damaged, reset, lost, or app data is deleted without a backup, your information may not be recoverable.

Data Retention

Artha retains information on your device until you delete it, uninstall the application, clear the application's local data, or restore/reset your device.

The developer does not maintain copies of your personal financial records or vault documents on developer-operated servers during normal use.

What Artha Does Not Do

Artha does not:

  • Read your SMS messages for financial tracking.
  • Require bank account linking.
  • Collect internet banking credentials.
  • Collect UPI PINs.
  • Collect card PINs.
  • Collect ATM PINs.
  • Ask for OTPs.
  • Upload your financial records to developer-operated servers by default.
  • Sell your personal financial information.
  • Use your financial records for targeted advertising by the developer.

Important Notice

Artha is a personal finance and records organization tool. It is not a bank, financial advisor, investment advisor, tax advisor, legal advisor, insurance advisor, or accounting service.

The app helps you organize information, but it does not guarantee financial outcomes, investment returns, tax accuracy, insurance coverage, legal compliance, or accounting accuracy.

You should independently verify important information and consult qualified professionals before making financial, legal, tax, investment, or insurance decisions.

Shared Responsibility

Artha is designed to protect your privacy by keeping your information under your control. At the same time, safe usage also depends on how you protect your device, backups, exported files, and shared information.

By using Artha, you understand that you are responsible for:

  • Keeping your device secure.
  • Protecting your app PIN or biometric access.
  • Maintaining backups.
  • Checking the accuracy of your records.
  • Reviewing files before exporting or sharing.
  • Using the app only for lawful personal purposes.
  • Respecting the privacy and rights of others when storing family, medical, financial, or personal records.

Applicable Laws

You are responsible for ensuring that your use of Artha complies with applicable laws and regulations in your location.
